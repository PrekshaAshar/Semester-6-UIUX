Net Art

Introduction to Net Art

Net art, short for "internet art," is a form of digital art that emerged in the 1990s, utilizing the internet as both its medium and venue. Unlike traditional art forms, net art is designed to exist and be experienced online, often exploring themes of interactivity, virtual space, and networked culture. It challenges conventional notions of authorship, audience, and distribution by offering interactive, collaborative, and often ephemeral experiences that blend art with technology.

Exercise: Researching Interactive Storytelling Websites

The objective of this exercise was to explore how storytelling can be transformed through interactive web-based platforms, focusing on elements such as user engagement, narrative flow, and technological execution. The process involved a detailed analysis of three distinct browser-based storytelling websites, examining how they employed interactivity to enhance their narrative experiences.

During the research phase, the purpose, functionality, message, and intentions of each website were critically evaluated. Additionally, the creators' backgrounds and the context of their creation were explored, providing insight into their creative processes. A significant emphasis was placed on understanding the technologies utilized, including HTML5, CSS, JavaScript, and WebGL, to identify how these tools contributed to the overall user experience.

From this research, several key insights emerged. Websites with minimalist designs effectively directed the user's attention to the narrative. Subtle animations and hover effects were identified as powerful tools to create immersion, while non-linear navigation made the experience feel more personalized and dynamic, allowing users to shape the story actively.

The user takes on the role of an observer, experiencing the story through a scroll-based interactive animation. The narrative follows a young Palestinian boy during wartime, depicting the slow collapse of his world as the war progresses. Through the use of parallax scrolling and animated transitions, the user witnesses his journey—starting with an ordinary day and leading to irreversible loss and displacement.
